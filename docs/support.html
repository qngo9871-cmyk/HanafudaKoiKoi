FAQ

How do I play?
Tap a card in your hand to play it. If it matches the month of a card on the field, you capture both. Then a card is drawn from the deck automatically and matched the same way.

What is "Koi-Koi"?
When you complete a scoring combination (a "yaku"), you can call Koi-Koi to keep playing for more points, or Shoubu to bank your score and end the hand. The further you push, the more you risk your opponent catching up.

What are Go-Stop and Hwatu?
Go-Stop is the Korean name for this same game, played with a Hwatu (flower card) deck. The rules in this app match the Japanese Koi-Koi tradition the cards originate from.

What are the difficulty levels?
Easy, Normal, and Hard control how well the AI opponent chooses captures and decides when to call Koi-Koi.
